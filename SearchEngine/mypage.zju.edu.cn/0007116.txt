', u'\r\n', u'\r\n\u3000 ', u'\r\n', u'\r\nChen Zhenlian ', u'\r\nVice Dean of School of Humanities, Zhejiang University ', u'\r\nChen Zhenlian, male, Han nationality, a native of Yinxian County, was born in Shanghai in 1956. In 1979, he was accepted to study at Zhejiang Academy of Fine Arts( presently China Academy of Art) and he studied Chinese calligraphy from such masters as Lu Weizhao, Sha Menghai and Shu Lesan and obtained his Master degree . Since 1993 he has worked as professor, doctorate supervisor in China Academy of Art. Since 2000, he has also worked as Vice Dean of School of Humanities, Director of Department of Art Science, Director of the Center for Chinese Art Studies, and Executive Vice Chair of the Committee of Culture and Arts, Zhejiang University, Executive Vice Chairman of the Association of Calligraphy and Painting, Zhejiang University. He is also doctorate supervisor in the field of Chinese calligraphy, painting & seal-engraving at Zhejiang University and doctorate supervisor in the field of Chinese calligraphy at China Academy of Art. ', u'\r\n', u'\r\nHis works of calligraphy, painting and seal-engraving have been shown in various foreign and domestic exhibitions and collected in some monographs. In Japan, Taiwan and the Mainland of China, he has held several exhibitions of his calligraphy, painting and seal-engraving, works and documents. He published Collected Works of Calligraphy, Painting and Seal Engraving by Chen Zhenlian, etc.. ', u'\r\n', u'\r\nHe focuses his study on theoretical studies of calligraphy, painting and seal-engraving. His academic publications include Exploration of the Beauty of the Forms of Traditional Chinese Painting, The Science of Calligraphy, The Aesthetic Science of Calligraphy, The Science of Calligraphy Education, The Modern History of Chinese Calligraphy, The Cultural History of Chinese Calligraphy, Introduction to Spatial Poetics, Aesthetic Study of the Schools of Ci during the Song Dynasty, Appraisal of Chinese Calligraphy, Painting and Seal-Engraving, A Comparative Study of Exchange History between Chinese Painting and Japanese Painting, etc. All of his forty academic books cover a wide scope of ancient Chinese poetry and Ci, Chinese painting, calligraphy & seal-engraving, Japanese calligraphy and painting, etc. Many of his papers have been published in such leading academic magazines as New Art, Art Research, and Literary and Art Studies, etc.. ', u'\r\n', u'\r\nIn terms of calligraphy creation, he advocates the creation pattern of academic school. In 1999, he won the title of \u201c 100 Top Young Artists\u201d awarded by China Federation of Literary and Art Circles. In terms of calligraphy theory, he advocates the academic study of the science of calligraphy. He is editor-in-chief of The Science of Calligraphy. In 1999, this book won the prize of the First National Art Science Achievement which was awarded by the Ministry of Culture and Lanting Award( the highest award in the field of calligraphy) in research section. In terms of calligraphy education, Prof Chen has created Chen Zhenlian\u2019s approach to teach calligraphy. The teaching approach won the National Prize in 1988. It is the first time to confer the prize to calligraphy education. In 1989, his approach won Huo Yingdong Fund Award. He compiled five college textbooks and edited fifteen college textbooks on calligraphy. In 2003, these textbooks won Lanting Award in education section. In terms of literary and art criticism, he won the Award of Contemporary Chinese Literary and Art Criticism.
